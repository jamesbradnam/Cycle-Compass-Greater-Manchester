OVERVIEW
============================================================
In Greater Manchester, there are several active travel options available to help you get into cycling, especially if you're considering e-bikes for commuting, leisure, or errands. One of the most accessible options is the "Borrow an E-bike" scheme, which allows residents over the age of 16 to try out an e-bike for free. This initiative, in partnership with Cycling UK, offers one-month loans from locations like Stretford Public Hall and the Community Hub in Stockport. E-bikes can make your journeys easier, especially in hilly areas, and are a great alternative for shorter trips or commuting without the hassle of arriving sweaty at work.

If you're looking for more options, there are various bike hire services across Greater Manchester. You can rent different types of bikes, including cargo bikes and city bikes, which can be a cost-effective way to explore cycling without the commitment of purchasing a bike. Additionally, bike libraries are available throughout the region, allowing you to borrow a bicycle like a book, giving you a chance to try cycling before deciding to invest in your own.

For those on a budget, there are also opportunities to get refurbished bikes at lower prices through local initiatives like the Manchester Bike Kitchen and other community-based cycle hire services. These options not only promote cycling as an environmentally friendly mode of transport but also help you save money.

Lastly, if you're interested in upgrading your cycling gear, consider the 'Bike to Work' scheme, which offers discounts on new bikes and accessories, making it easier to invest in your cycling journey. With these resources, Greater Manchester provides a supportive environment for anyone looking to embrace cycling as a practical and enjoyable way to travel.

RECOMMENDATIONS
============================================================
1. **Borrow an E-Bike**  
   You can try an e-bike for free through the TfGM scheme, which is perfect for testing out e-cycling for commuting and errands.  
   [Borrow an e-bike](https://beeactive.tfgm.com/borrow-an-ebike)

2. **One-Month E-Cycle Loan**  
   Take advantage of the one-month e-cycle loan offered by Cycling UK to see how an e-bike fits into your daily routine without any upfront cost.  
   [Borrow an e-bike booking](https://www.cyclinguk.org/borrow-ebike)

3. **Explore Bike Libraries**  
   Visit a local bike library to borrow a bicycle for free and try out different types, including e-bikes, before making a purchase.  
   [Bike libraries](https://beeactive.tfgm.com/cycling/get-a-bike)

4. **Get a Refurbished Bike**  
   Consider getting a refurbished bike from local shops, which can be a low-cost alternative to buying new, allowing you to start cycling without breaking the bank.  
   [Get a bike - Access to bikes](https://beeactive.tfgm.com/cycling/get-a-bike)

5. **Bike to Work Scheme**  
   If your employer offers it, join the 'Bike to Work' scheme to get up to 40% off a new bike and accessories, making it easier to afford your cycling needs.  
   [Bike to work scheme](https://beeactive.tfgm.com/cycling/get-a-bike)
